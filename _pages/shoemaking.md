A short story about how I became a Master shoemaker

As a kid I discovered that the work in the hoppy workshop of my dad filled me with pleasure. Long summer holidays out on the countryside gave little distraction and the workshop was a place where my creativity could flourish. When I graduated school and had the world of opportunities lying in front of me, I found a book about besppoke shoemaking in the library of my dad. I remember untilt oday that I opened the book and had to confess that I knew so little about leather and had absolutely no idea about how to make a shoe. To this point I had gathered quite some expereince with wood and was confident building large structues but also furniture etc.

Hence, I decided to persue a apprenticeship in a bespoke shoemaking workshop 45 min drive from home in Neumünster. While bespoke shoemaker had already been a rare species of craftsman, Neumünster had two, which seemed werid at first. But the history of Neumünster is very industrial and it used to be a center of textile and leather prodcution in the second half of the 20th century. being close to the tanners and factories that can supply you as a craftsman with tools and material is definetly an advantage. 

I started as an intern in 2012 together with Hans-Joachim Vauk who was a real Master of his craft with experience in at Bally in Swizzerland (who did absolutely first class hand crafted bespoke shoes in the 70s) and had run a business for over 40 years when I started. His eye for perfection, his calm hand with any kind of tool or machine he used just amazed me. Like it amazes me when someone gives a really good talk or write a really good paragraph in a paper. Skill and perfection can be learned by a lot of practice and Hans-Joachim was perfection for me. 

I started my apprenticeship and finished as best of my class in 2014 with my Gesellenstück which is a pair of shoe I had to finsih within a week to not only show perfection but also speed and economical working style. 

While working at the workbench sitting many hours a day cutting, clicking, skiving, stitching, lasting, welting, sole attachement, heel building, edge trimming and polishing I listend every day to the german radio channel "Deutschlandfunk - Forschung aktuell" (german radio channel, sciene today or science update) which was always a highlight of the day. I knew before I started this apprenticeship that I wanted to study one day at a University as well, like all my friends did. the radio channel "Foschung aktuell" helped me decide it shall be physics.

Until today, two hearts beat in my chest: one for science and one for the crafts. It is almost as if these two have to keep a balance. After long working weeks at the desk I need to do something at the work bench, use my hands to clean the mental disk space and to restart motivatedly the next week at the desk and vice versa. I don't see these two interests of mine as competing disciplines but rather as each other supporting. Some people hop on a bike and race around the country side, others read and go for walks, I go into the workshop, feel the leather and enjoy using my tools.

However, after having finished both my PhD and my Masters degree in shoemaking I decided that my future lies in physics, espeically atmospheric phyiscs with a focus und climate change and extremes as I believe that my impact on society is bigger and I can help more people with that.
